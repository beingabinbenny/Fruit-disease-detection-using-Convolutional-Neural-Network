Diseases in Mango

  • Alternaria
  • Anthracnose
  • Black Mould Rot
  • Stem and Rot

Alternaria

The fungus (germ) causes black spots and post harvest fruit rot during storage. The germ infects the fruit during growing stage and becomes inactive. When fruit is harvested and as the fruit continues to ripen, the germ resumes growth resulting in an active rot.

Anthracnose

Mango anthracnose disease (MAD) is a worldwide disease that is extremely destructive to mangoes before and after harvest. The disease damages the infected mango trees, leading to low yield and quality of fruits.

Black Mould Rot

Greenish-black or grayish-green mould growth on the fruit's surface. As the disease progresses, the infected areas become sunken or depressed, often with a soft, decayed texture beneath the mouldy surface. Affected fruits may emit a characteristic musty or moldy smell.

Stem and Rot

A dark rot develops from the stem end as fruit ripen after harvest. A dark brown to black rot begins at the stem end as a dark brown ring and the rot proceeds towards the other end. The rot produces dark streaking of the water-conducting tissues (this symptom distinguishes stem end rot from anthracnose).
